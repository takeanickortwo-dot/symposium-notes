PROLOGUE: THE CALLING

The mountain was no longer Olympus. It was a mountain of the mind—a place where time folded upon itself, where Lucy's bones could speak and Columbus's maps could be unfurled beside the clay tablets of Sumer. A voice, not a god, but the collective weight of all questions ever asked, summoned them:

VOICE OF THE AGES
"You who have shaped or been shaped by what men call civilization—come. Speak. Disagree. The living listen."

And so they came: from the rift valley, from the Nile, from the Euphrates, from the Indus, from the Yellow River, from the Andes and the Mexican highlands, from the forests of Gaul and the steppes of Asia, from the decks of caravels and the cabinets of kings. They came not as ghosts, but as themselves—as they were when they were most themselves.
